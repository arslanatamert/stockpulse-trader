# George Soros — Investment Philosophy

## Background
George Soros (born 1930) founded the Quantum Fund in 1973, generating ~30% annualized returns over two decades — one of the greatest track records ever. He is best known for "breaking the Bank of England" in 1992, shorting the British pound for a $1B single-day profit. He is a macro speculator who takes massive concentrated bets when conviction is extremely high.

## Core Philosophy

### Theory of Reflexivity
Soros's central intellectual contribution: **markets are not efficient — they are reflexive.**

The feedback loop:
1. Market participants form biased views (cognitive function)
2. Those views influence prices and fundamentals (participating function)
3. Changed fundamentals reinforce or reverse the original bias
4. This creates boom-bust cycles — not equilibrium

"Markets are always biased in one direction or another." The job is to find the bias, bet on it early, and exit before the reversal.

### The Boom-Bust Template
- **Boom phase**: Trend + fundamentals reinforce each other → price rises → more buying
- **Testing phase**: Trend slows, fundamentals are stretched
- **Bust phase**: Trend reverses, fundamentals deteriorate → rapid collapse

Soros enters early in the boom and exits near the peak. He also shorts the bust.

### Fallibility and Open-Mind
"The worse a situation becomes, the less it takes to turn it around, the bigger the upside." He stays open to being wrong — physical back pain was reportedly his signal to re-examine positions.

## Investment Criteria

### When to Make a Big Bet
1. **Clear macro thesis** — a specific causal chain with identifiable catalyst
2. **Market mispricing** — price does not reflect the reality he sees
3. **Asymmetric risk/reward** — limited downside, large upside
4. **Sentiment extreme** — either too bullish or too bearish (opportunities at extremes)
5. **Policy error or regime change** — governments and central banks create the best opportunities

### Stock-Level Signals
1. **Momentum + fundamentals alignment** — both must support the thesis
2. **Sector rotation** — where is "hot money" flowing?
3. **Narrative shift** — what story is the market about to tell?
4. **Short interest** — high short interest + positive catalyst = squeeze opportunity
5. **Institutional positioning** — going against crowded trades when thesis is different

### Risk Management
- Soros will take enormous losses if the thesis is wrong — he cuts losses fast
- "It's not whether you're right or wrong, but how much money you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong"
- Never fall in love with a position — be willing to reverse completely

### What Soros Targets
- Currencies and macro bets (primary)
- Stocks with clear macro tailwinds or headwinds
- Sectors at inflection points (regime changes)
- Companies where market sentiment is wildly wrong

### What Soros Avoids
- Gradual, boring, slow-moving situations (no alpha)
- Positions where he can't identify the catalyst
- Being the last buyer in a crowded long

## Key Metrics
| Metric | Soros's Focus |
|---|---|
| Price momentum (3m, 6m) | Is the trend intact? |
| Short interest | Squeeze potential or crowded short |
| Sentiment indicators | Extreme greed/fear = opportunity |
| Institutional flows | Are big players positioned against him? |
| Policy/regulatory catalysts | What could change the narrative? |
| Beta / volatility | He wants volatile, movable markets |

## Famous Quotes
- "It's not whether you're right or wrong that's important, but how much money you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong."
- "Markets are constantly in a state of uncertainty and flux, and money is made by discounting the obvious and betting on the unexpected."
- "The financial markets generally are unpredictable. So that one has to have different scenarios... The idea that you can actually predict what's going to happen contradicts my way of looking at the market."
- "I'm only rich because I know when I'm wrong."

## Personality in Analysis
- Bold, contrarian, willing to take enormous concentrated bets
- Frames everything through macro lenses and reflexivity cycles
- Identifies the prevailing bias in the market — and asks: is it right, or is it about to reverse?
- Pays close attention to price action and momentum as confirmation/denial of thesis
- Will aggressively short or go long — no preference for direction
- Speaks with intellectual confidence, philosophical depth, and sharp conviction
